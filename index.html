How to Choose the Best Networking Technology for Very Remote IoT Applications
Trending Webinars, How To, Webinars, < 30 Minutes

Expand your knowledge on network solutions for remote IoT applications.

Run Time: 15 Minutes

In this IoT Central webinar, you will expand your knowledge on network solutions for remote IoT applications. We delve into the world of Satellite-Enabled Low Power Wide Area Networks (LPWAN) and explore which network to choose, when, and why.

We'll explore how satellite LPWAN solutions come into play for extremely remote sensors beyond the reach of cellular infrastructure. Comparing two main approaches: using unlicensed LPWAN technologies (e.g., LoRaWAN, mioty, Sigfox) with satellite backhaul or connecting sensors individually to a satellite transceiver.

You'll learn about real-world use cases, including tsunami warning systems and other mission-critical applications utilizing satellite LPWAN, and gain insights into the practical considerations for choosing between LoRaWAN and individual satellite connections based on geographical and operational factors.

We also discuss developing technology of NTN NB-IoT and its potential to open up new use cases by enabling seamless switching between cellular and satellite networks. We cover the anticipated cost benefits and new use cases emerging from this advancement.

You should attend if you'd like to:

- Enhance your expertise in IoT networking solutions.
- Stay ahead of industry trends and technological advancements.
- Gain practical knowledge to make informed decisions for your remote IoT projects.

Don't miss this opportunity to broaden your understanding of satellite-enabled LPWAN and its impact on remote IoT applications.

Speaker: Oliver Potter, COO at Ground Control

We discuss how secure, reliable connectivity is paving the way for EV adoption.

Length: 42 minutes

In this IoT Central webinar, we will discuss how secure, reliable connectivity is paving the way for electric vehicle (EV) adoption. The risks of downtime or poor cellular connectivity can cause a significant loss of revenue for EVs and EV charging operators. Moreover, the more EVs and charging stations there are, the greater the attack surface is for hackers to take advantage.

However, reliable remote connectivity can ensure EVs enable a smooth, stable exchange between EVs and the power grid. In addition, firmware over-the-air (FOTA) updates can repair bugs and update security for cyberattack protection.

Join Telit Cinterion to explore the benefits and challenges of IoT connectivity for EVs and EV charging stations, and the role secure, reliable communications play in the Ev Charging Ecosystem.

In this webinar, you will learn:
The connectivity obstacles EV charging faces and how to overcome them
The technology and design considerations necessary to future-proof the EV charging infrastructure
The importance of Always on Connectivity for critical infrastructure

Watch this on demand webinar today to understand the important role of connectivity for the future of EVs.
